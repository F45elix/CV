My fascination with AI didn't start in a lecture hall — it started the moment I realised machines could learn to think faster than attackers can move. During my attachment at the Attorney General's Office, I watched threats evolve in real time.

That experience planted a question I haven't stopped chasing — what if defence could be as intelligent as the attack? Every firewall rule, every anomaly flagged, every identity verified is a small act of protecting real people.

Pursuing my MSc in Cybersecurity at the University of Chester is a deliberate move toward the intersection of machine learning and threat intelligence. I study network behaviour not just to fix it, but to teach a system to read it automatically.

Adversaries leverage AI to craft more convincing attacks. My conviction: the most resilient defences of tomorrow will themselves be intelligent. I want to build those defences.
